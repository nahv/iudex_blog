# Iudex — Premium Website Redesign Proposal

**Audience:** abogados y juzgados, Corrientes (AR)
**Scope:** full redesign strategy, pre-implementation
**Tone:** modern, premium, timeless — Apple 2010-era discipline applied to legal intelligence
**Status:** strategy only. No code, no visuals. Thinking first.

---

## 1. Conceptual Direction

### Concept: **"Estudio de Inteligencia Jurídica"**

A studio, not a tool. An observatory, not a search box.

Most legal-tech websites fall into one of three traps:

1. **Notary aesthetic** — marble, columns, serif overload, burgundy and gold. Signals "old, trustworthy, slow".
2. **SaaS hype** — gradients, neon, dashboard screenshots, "10x your firm". Signals "loud, generic, temporary".
3. **Utility minimalism** — database-style UI, cold, engineered. Signals "this is a database, not a practice".

Iudex sits in a different quadrant: **calm intelligence**. The design metaphor is a studio — the place where a professional thinks, researches, connects, and produces. Not a library (passive), not a dashboard (reactive), not a tool (instrumental). A studio: where the work is done with care.

### Why this differentiates

- **From old-law sites**: we replace ornament with clarity. Serif typography earns its place through rhythm and restraint, not decoration.
- **From SaaS competitors**: we replace product screenshots with *demonstrations of thinking*. You don't see the UI first — you see the shift in work.
- **From generic tech minimalism**: we use warmth (cream, ink, gold) and argentinian specificity. Iudex is *from Corrientes*, not from Silicon Valley.

### One-sentence frame

> Iudex no es un buscador de jurisprudencia. Es el estudio donde el derecho se piensa.

That sentence is the brief for every design decision.

## 2. Scrollytelling Structure (Homepage Narrative)

The homepage is a seven-act scroll. Each act is a single idea. The scroll is *earned*, not padded — if a section doesn't carry a load, it doesn't exist.

Motion philosophy: transitions happen between sections, never *within* them once visible. Nothing distracting. No parallax-for-parallax's-sake. Think of it as turning the pages of a very well-designed book.

### Act I — Hero: **The Statement**

- **Purpose:** first impression. No hedging. No "plataforma líder de…". A declaration.
- **Key message:** Iudex is a change in how law is practiced.
- **Visual idea:** full-bleed, high-contrast. Ink on cream. One line of display type, dominant. A single kinetic element — maybe a legal citation being *composed* on-screen (letter by letter, then settling). No hero image of smiling lawyers. No dashboard mockup.
- **Microcopy (ES):**
  > **"El estudio donde el derecho se piensa."**
  > Investigar, conectar, decidir. En un solo lugar.
- **Subtle action:** one CTA, "Ver cómo funciona" — which triggers scroll, not a modal. The only primary action on the fold.

### Act II — Problem Framing: **The Weight**

- **Purpose:** earn the reader's attention by naming their reality precisely.
- **Key message:** el trabajo del abogado está fragmentado. Buscar, leer, anotar, copiar, redactar — cada tarea en un sistema distinto.
- **Visual idea:** a split, static composition. Left column: a list of the tools a lawyer juggles today (navegador, PDFs, Word, Excel de expedientes, WhatsApp, correo, SAIJ, sistema provincial…). Right column: quiet space. The imbalance is the message.
- **Microcopy (ES):**
  > **"Hoy el derecho se escribe entre doce pestañas."**
  > El tiempo no se va en pensar. Se va en cambiar de pantalla.
- **Transition out:** as the user scrolls, the list on the left fades down one at a time — literally compressing.

### Act III — The Paradigm Shift: **Old / New**

- **Purpose:** the pivot. This is where the story turns.
- **Key message:** el trabajo jurídico deja de ser *buscar resultados* y pasa a ser *investigar problemas*.
- **Visual idea:** a two-column structural contrast. Not a comparison table (too corporate). Two columns of running prose, different typographic weight.
  - **Antes:** problema → búsqueda → resultados → lectura → redacción. Lineal, fragmentado.
  - **Ahora:** problema → investigación asistida → conexiones → síntesis → producción. Circular, conectado.
- **Microcopy (ES):**
  > **"Del buscar al investigar."**
  > Antes empezabas con una palabra clave. Ahora empezás con una pregunta.
- **Motion:** the word "buscar" is struck through softly and replaced by "investigar" as the section enters the viewport. Used once, with intent.

### Act IV — Product Intelligence Reveal: **The Instrument**

- **Purpose:** the "how". Reveal the product without showing a dashboard.
- **Key message:** Iudex es un instrumento de investigación jurídica que entiende contexto, no solo términos.
- **Visual idea:** a composed vignette of *the thinking process*, not the UI. Show a legal question being entered in plain language → snippets of jurisprudencia, doctrina, y normativa organizándose alrededor → líneas finas conectando fragmentos → una cita emergiendo. Restrained animation, one beat at a time. No screenshots of the actual product yet.
- **Microcopy (ES):**
  > **"Empezás con una pregunta. Terminás con un argumento."**
  > Iudex investiga con vos. No por vos.
- **Tone:** the "no por vos" matters. Lawyers distrust black-box AI. We state the limit of the machine.

### Act V — Workflow Visualization: **The Studio**

- **Purpose:** show the product in situ. This is the only section that may show actual UI.
- **Key message:** expedientes, plantillas, investigación, y salida jurídica — un solo espacio.
- **Visual idea:** a single, wide, full-bleed canvas shot (not a carousel, not a grid of screenshots). The canvas scrolls *horizontally* as the user scrolls vertically — a scroll-jack done with restraint. As the horizontal scroll advances, four anchor points are named: *Problema*, *Investigación*, *Conexión*, *Producción*. Each anchor has one line of copy.
- **Microcopy (ES) — anchors:**
  > **Problema.** Definí el caso, no el query.
  > **Investigación.** Jurisprudencia, doctrina y normativa, en un mismo hilo.
  > **Conexión.** Lo que leés, lo que escribís, lo que archivás — conectado.
  > **Producción.** Tu escrito nace del trabajo, no de una plantilla vacía.
- **Discipline:** no feature list. No badges. No "✓". Four sentences, one canvas.

### Act VI — Trust & Credibility: **Who Stands Behind**

- **Purpose:** the trust beat. Corrientes lawyers don't buy from strangers.
- **Key message:** Iudex está hecho desde adentro de la práctica jurídica argentina.
- **Visual idea:** not logos-of-firms. A short block of text — founder's sentence, or an institutional quote — set large, in serif, like an epigraph. Below: discreet facts (años, fuentes integradas, juzgados, casos procesados) in mono type, small caps. No vanity metrics.
- **Microcopy (ES):**
  > **"Lo construimos como lo hubiéramos querido usar."**
  > — *Equipo Iudex, Corrientes, Argentina.*
- **Motion:** none. This section stands still. Gravity.

### Act VII — Final CTA: **The Invitation**

- **Purpose:** close with confidence.
- **Key message:** probalo.
- **Visual idea:** massive whitespace. One line of copy. One button. No form on the homepage. The form lives on the next page, so the commitment to click is small.
- **Microcopy (ES):**
  > **"Abrí el estudio."**
  > [Empezar →]
- **Post-CTA:** footer is quiet — contacto, about, blog. Nothing else.

## 3. Communication Improvements (Before vs After)

### Principle: replace legal-ese and SaaS-ese with precision.

Two failure modes dominate legal websites: over-formal ("brindamos soluciones integrales para el ejercicio profesional") and over-hyped ("revolucioná tu estudio con IA"). Both hide the product.

The rule: **if a sentence could appear on any competitor's site, rewrite it.**

### For lawyers (abogados)

| Vector | Before (generic / bad) | After (Iudex) |
|---|---|---|
| Value prop | "Optimizá tu trabajo con inteligencia artificial" | "Investigá un caso en el tiempo que hoy te lleva abrir los buscadores" |
| Feature framing | "Potente motor de búsqueda de jurisprudencia" | "Pregunta en lenguaje natural. Respuesta con fuentes citadas" |
| Time saving | "Ahorrá horas de trabajo" | "El escrito nace de la investigación, no en paralelo" |
| Trust | "Tecnología confiable para profesionales" | "Cada cita es rastreable. Ninguna respuesta sin fuente" |
| Onboarding | "Registrate en minutos" | "Tu primer caso investigado, en diez minutos" |

Common thread: replace adjectives with specifics. "Potente" says nothing. "Diez minutos" says everything.

### For court teams (juzgados)

The messaging for juzgados is almost entirely missing from current legal-tech marketing — everyone speaks to abogados. This is a competitive opening.

| Vector | Before (generic / bad) | After (Iudex) |
|---|---|---|
| Value prop | "Soluciones para el poder judicial" | "Trazabilidad de criterio. Consistencia entre expedientes. Sin fricción con los sistemas existentes" |
| Institutional angle | "Mejoramos la eficiencia" | "Cada decisión es consultable. Cada criterio, documentado" |
| Integration | "Integración con sistemas judiciales" | "Convive con los sistemas provinciales. No los reemplaza" |
| Trust | "Seguridad y confidencialidad" | "Los datos no salen del juzgado. La inteligencia sí entra" |

Common thread: for juzgados, speak to *institutional memory* and *criterio*, not productivity. Juzgados don't want to go faster. They want to be consistent and traceable.

### Tone rules (both audiences)

- **Never** use the words: "revolucionario", "disruptivo", "potente", "solución integral", "plataforma líder".
- **Prefer** precise verbs: investigar, conectar, producir, decidir, rastrear.
- **Use numbers** only when they name a reality, not when they sell a claim.
- **Address the reader as an equal**, not a prospect. No "vos también podés".

## 4. Interaction & UX Patterns

### Navigation philosophy: **minimal, structured when needed**

The homepage has no visible top navigation during Act I. As the user scrolls past the hero, a minimal horizontal navbar fades in — four items, no dropdowns: *Producto · Para juzgados · Historias · Contacto*. That's it.

Why: the first beat should feel like a statement, not a site. The navbar arrives once the reader has decided to stay.

### Command palette as a secondary entry

Introduce a **⌘K command palette** — not for the marketing site itself, but as a *signature element of the product brand*. On the homepage, show it once, briefly, in Act IV ("Empezás con una pregunta"). The palette is an idiom that signals "this product thinks the way you think".

On the marketing site itself, ⌘K can act as a quiet way to jump between sections/pages. It's a flex that costs almost nothing and telegraphs craft.

### Microinteractions (a short, strict list)

- **Link hover:** 150ms color shift, gold underline drawing in left-to-right. No bounce.
- **Scroll reveal:** sections fade in at 8% opacity → 100% over 400ms, slight y-translate (8px). Once, never again.
- **Text kinetic moments:** *one per page maximum*. (e.g., the "buscar → investigar" strike-through in Act III.)
- **Buttons:** solid ink on cream. On hover, the button *doesn't* change color — the cursor does, and a gold caret appears to the right. Apple-era restraint.
- **Cursor:** in Act V's horizontal canvas, the cursor becomes a small plus sign to signal pan behavior. Nowhere else.

### Making the product feel intelligent without being noisy

- **Citations everywhere.** Any AI-style demo snippet shows its source. Visible footnote, legible.
- **No chat bubble.** Do not put a ChatGPT-like widget on the homepage. It cheapens the product and misrepresents it.
- **Confidence through silence.** Empty space is the visual signal for "we've thought about this". Most legal-tech sites signal intelligence by stuffing the page. We signal it by removing.
- **Absence of stock AI imagery.** No brains, no circuits, no glowing orbs. The product is not a chatbot.

## 5. Layout & UI System Direction

### Grid

12-column grid, 1440px max content width, 72px side gutters on desktop. Mobile: 4 columns, 24px gutters. The grid is *breathable* — most content lives on 6–8 columns centered, with the remaining columns as margin. Full-bleed is reserved for hero and Act V canvas.

### Typography scale

Anchor the system on the three families already in use (Playfair Display / DM Sans / DM Mono), but tighten the scale.

- **Display XL** — 96px / 1.05 / -2% tracking — Playfair. Used once per page, in hero.
- **Display L** — 64px / 1.1 — Playfair. Act titles.
- **Heading M** — 40px / 1.2 — Playfair.
- **Body L** — 20px / 1.55 — DM Sans. Primary reading size on desktop. Generous.
- **Body M** — 16px / 1.6 — DM Sans. Secondary.
- **Label** — 12px / 1.2 / uppercase / 8% tracking — DM Mono. Used for anchors, metadata, timestamps.

Discipline: no size between these. If a heading doesn't fit one of four sizes, the design is wrong, not the scale.

### Color

Keep the current ink / cream / gold system. Suggested refinement:

- **Ink** remains the dominant type color — deep, near-black with a warm undertone (avoid pure #000).
- **Cream** is the default background for most of the page.
- **Gold** is reserved for *one thing per page*: the primary CTA or a single semantic accent (e.g., a cited phrase). Overusing gold destroys its power.
- **Introduce a "paper" tone** — a slightly cooler cream (almost white with a hint of gray) — for Act V canvas sections, to visually separate product surfaces from marketing surfaces.
- **No dark mode on the marketing site.** Iudex's identity is light, considered, and printable.

### Full-bleed vs cards vs canvas

- **Full-bleed** — hero, Act V canvas, final CTA. Used when the message is the space.
- **Canvas** (large, single framed illustration) — Act IV product reveal. Used to show thinking.
- **Cards** — *minimized*. Used only on the blog index and "historias" page. Never on the homepage. Cards are a crutch that make every SaaS homepage look the same.

### How to avoid template-looking design

- **No three-column feature grid.** Ever.
- **No icon-above-heading-above-paragraph triplets.** Ever.
- **No decorative gradients.** Color transitions, if any, happen on very large surfaces, very slowly.
- **One typographic rhythm per page.** Don't mix sans and serif in the same paragraph.
- **Asymmetric compositions.** Centered layouts on every section kills distinction. Alternate: centered hero, left-weighted Acts II/III, right-weighted Acts IV/V, centered CTA.
- **Original illustration, not stock.** If visuals are needed, commission one tight illustration system (line-based, ink-on-cream) rather than licensing stock.

## 6. Signature Elements (Uniqueness)

Three ideas that, once seen, would make Iudex recognizable without the logo.

### A. The **Pulso Jurídico**

A small, always-present element in the top-right of the marketing site: a single thin vertical line that rises and falls gently, like a seismograph — tied to a real data signal (for instance, *volume of new jurisprudencia indexed this week* in Argentina). Hover reveals a tooltip: "Esta semana se incorporaron N fallos a Iudex. Última actualización: [timestamp]."

Why it works:
- It makes the platform feel *alive* without being loud.
- It telegraphs coverage depth without claiming it.
- It's a concrete, data-backed signature — not a decoration.

### B. The **Cita que se compone**

On the hero, a single legal citation assembles itself on screen (once, on page load, never again) — letter by letter or fragment by fragment, like a secretary or a clerk typing it out. The chosen citation is rotated daily from a curated list of landmark Argentine rulings, with a small attribution below. It's a micro-ritual: every day, the homepage opens with a different piece of living Argentine law.

Why it works:
- It positions Iudex inside the legal tradition, not outside it.
- It turns the hero into a place worth revisiting.
- It's entirely "brand theater" — it doesn't sell the product, it performs the culture.

### C. The **Conexión visible**

In Act IV's product reveal, when a legal question is entered, do not show a list of results. Show a small, calm visualization of *fragments connecting*: a fallo pulls in a doctrina note, which in turn pulls in an article of normativa, thin lines drawing between them. No network-graph overkill — maybe six nodes total, connected deliberately, with a clear narrative order.

Why it works:
- It makes *connection* — the real product differentiator — visible.
- It avoids the trap of showing a search-results list, which would reduce Iudex back to "a fancier SAIJ".
- It's a visual idiom that can be reused across product surfaces (product UI, blog post diagrams, pitch deck).

## 7. Summary (How to decide)

When in doubt, apply these three tests to any design choice:

1. **The competitor test.** Could this section appear on a generic legal-SaaS site with the logo swapped? If yes, cut or rewrite.
2. **The silence test.** If we remove a visual element, does the section say *less* or say *more clearly*? If "more clearly", remove.
3. **The lawyer test.** Would a senior Corrientes lawyer, reading this copy in 15 seconds, feel respected — or sold to? If sold to, rewrite.

2. Lock the seven-act homepage narrative. Cut acts if necessary — the document can sustain six, not more.

5. Prototype Act I only. Hero + transition to Act II. Validate the feel before expanding.
6. Only after the prototype lands, continue with Acts II–VII.

Do the first three without writing a line of code. The redesign lives or dies at the concept + copy stage.
